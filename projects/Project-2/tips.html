Tips and Tricks

  • Practice on Carpet or Grass

    Practicing on carpet or grass with your deck helps beginners get comfortable with balancing on a skateboard without the board rolling away. The softer surface provides more grip and stability, making it easier to focus on mastering basic movements like standing, balancing, and foot placement before moving onto smoother, faster surfaces.

  • Practice Taking Falls

    Falling is part of skateboarding, so stay loose and try to roll when you hit the ground to spread the impact, avoiding stiff arms or hands that could lead to injury. Bend your knees and aim to absorb the fall with your side or shoulder, and always wear protective gear to keep yourself safe while learning.

  • Use You Shoulders to Stay Balanced

    Your shoulders can really affect how balanced you are during an Ollie. As you set up for the trick, keep your shoulders squared with the board.
